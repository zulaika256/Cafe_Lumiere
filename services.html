Safety and Well-Being

Your child's well-being is paramount to us, and we take every measure to ensure it.
  • Regular Health Check-Ups: We perform regular health check-ups and ensure your child’s overall health is maintained.
  • Strict Safety Protocols and Monitoring: Our staff is trained to follow strict safety protocols, including emergency drills, first-aid practices, and constant supervision.
  • Emotional and Social Support: We provide a nurturing environment that supports emotional growth and social development. Through our activities, children learn how to express their feelings, build social connections, and resolve conflicts peacefully.

Mental and Emotional Well-Being

Supporting your child’s mental and emotional health is an essential part of our curriculum.
  • Counseling Services: We offer individual or group counseling sessions to address any emotional or behavioral challenges your child may face.
  • Mindfulness Activities: Regular mindfulness practices such as guided meditation, deep breathing exercises, and yoga to help children develop emotional regulation and stress management skills.
  • Positive Behavior Reinforcement: We emphasize the importance of positive behavior through praise, rewards, and creating a safe space for children to learn how to manage their emotions constructively.

Nutrition and Physical Activity

Healthy nutrition and physical activity are key components of our curriculum.
  • Healthy Meals and Snacks: We provide nutritious, balanced meals and snacks throughout the day. Children enjoy a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and protein-rich foods, with options to accommodate dietary restrictions and preferences.
  • Outdoor Play and Physical Education: Physical activity is an essential part of the day. Children engage in outdoor play, structured games, and physical exercises designed to promote their health, coordination, and teamwork.
  • Nutrition Education: We introduce children to the importance of healthy eating through fun lessons and hands-on activities, such as creating their own healthy snacks and learning about food groups.

Well-Being Policies

Our policies are designed to maintain a safe and healthy environment for all children.
  • Child Supervision: Our staff maintains a high staff-to-child ratio to ensure every child is closely supervised and supported throughout the day.
  • Inclusion and Support for Special Needs: We accommodate children with special needs through personalized care and inclusive activities, ensuring each child has the resources they need to thrive.
  • Emergency Response Plan: We have a clear emergency response plan in place, which is practiced regularly to ensure both staff and children are prepared in case of any emergency.
Kindergarten Policy
Our Kindergarten Policy
    1. Child Safety and Supervision
      • We maintain a safe staff-to-child ratio and require children to be signed in and out by a parent or authorized guardian.
      • Emergency contact and medical information must be provided at enrollment.
    2. Health and Hygiene
      • Children should stay home if sick. We maintain cleanliness by sanitizing regularly.
      • Parents must inform us of any allergies or special medical needs.
    3. Behavior and Discipline
      • We use positive reinforcement and redirection for behavior management.
      • Aggressive behavior will involve parent discussions and a behavior plan.
    4. Curriculum and Activities
      • Our curriculum promotes learning through play, art, outdoor activities, and motor skills.
      • Regular progress updates will be shared with parents.
    5. Parental Involvement
      • Parents are encouraged to attend meetings, volunteer, and communicate with teachers.
      • Concerns or questions should be addressed promptly.
    6. Meals and Snacks
      • Healthy meals and snacks are provided. Parents must inform us of any food allergies or restrictions.
    7. Special Needs and Inclusion
      • We accommodate special needs through personalized support and inclusive activities.
    8. Field Trips and Extra-Curricular Activities
      • Field trips will require parent permission. Extra-curricular activities will be communicated in advance.
    9. Privacy and Confidentiality
      • All personal and medical information is confidential. Photos or videos will not be shared without consent.
    10. Hours of Operation and Attendance
      • Our hours are 8am to 3pm, Monday until Friday. Parents must inform us of absences or late arrivals.
